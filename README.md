**About the Project**

This project presents a complete analysis of sales data from a retail e-commerce company specialized in home decoration and lifestyle gift items. The objective was to create a professional interactive dashboard to identify business growth and optimization opportunities.

### 🎯 Business Objectives

* ✅ **Analyze revenue evolution:** Identify trends, seasonality, and critical periods
* ✅ **Optimize the product catalog:** Identify best-sellers and low-turnover products
* ✅ **Geographic expansion:** Analyze current markets and identify expansion opportunities
* ✅ **Improve customer retention:** Calculate customer KPIs and propose concrete actions
* ✅ **Provide actionable recommendations:** Prioritized action plan with estimated ROI

### 📊 Dataset

* **Source:** 🛒 Online Retail Dataset (Kaggle)
  [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ulrikthygepedersen/online-retail-dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ulrikthygepedersen/online-retail-dataset)
  Real e-commerce transactions with product, quantity, and revenue data.

* **Period:** December 2010 – December 2011 (13 months)

* **Volume:** ~540,000 transactions

* **Coverage:** 38 countries, 4,300+ unique customers, 3,900+ products

* **Columns:** InvoiceNo, StockCode, Description, Quantity, InvoiceDate, UnitPrice, CustomerID, Country

## 💡 **Analysis and Insights**

### 🎄 1. Strong Sales Seasonality (Impact: Critical)

**📊 Key Data:**

* Q4 (Oct–Dec): **65% of annual revenue** (£6.3M)
* November: **Peak at £1.5M** (+89% vs October)
* January: **Drop of -35%** compared to December

**🔍 Analysis:**
The “gifts and decoration” positioning creates excessive dependence on the Christmas period. This concentration generates major operational and financial risks.

**💥 Business Impact:**

* ⚠️ Cash flow risk in Q1–Q2 (January–June)
* ⚠️ High financing needs in Q3 to build inventory
* ⚠️ Supply chain pressure in October–November
* ⚠️ Resource underutilization (teams, warehouse) 7 months/year

**✅ Recommendations:**

**Short Term (0–3 months):**

1. Launch a “New Year Resolutions” campaign in January (organization, wellness items)
2. Create a Valentine’s Day collection (Jan 15 – Feb 14)
3. Targeted promotions on non-seasonal items in January (-20%)
4. Promote gift cards after Christmas

**Mid Term (3–12 months):**

1. Develop **4 themed collections**:

   * 🌸 Spring/Easter (March–April)
   * 💒 Wedding/Summer (May–July)
   * 🎃 Back-to-school/Halloween (Sept–Oct)
   * 🎄 Christmas (Nov–Dec)
2. Negotiate supplier payment facilities (spread Q3 → Q1)
3. Loyalty program to retain post-Christmas customers
4. Invest in the “home decoration” segment (less seasonal)

**Long Term (1–3 years):**

1. Diversify product mix: 30% permanent decoration / 70% seasonal
2. Develop B2B offering (hotels, restaurants, offices)
3. International expansion (different seasonal patterns)

**💰 Estimated ROI:** +12% to +18% annual revenue, +25% improved cash flow

### 🏆 2. Concentration on Best-Sellers (Opportunity: High)

**📊 Key Data:**

* **Top 10 products:** 25% of revenue (£2.4M)
* **Top 3:**

  1. REGENCY CAKESTAND 3 TIER (£164K)
  2. WHITE HANGING HEART T-LIGHT HOLDER (£95K)
  3. JUMBO BAG RED RETROSPOT (£93K)
* **Average price Top 10:** £2–£20 (affordable range)

**🔍 Analysis:**
The Pareto principle is confirmed. Best-sellers are mainly mid-range decoration items with strong repurchase potential.

**✅ Identified Opportunities:**

* ✅ Likely high margins (premium products)
* ✅ Variant potential (colors, sizes, materials)
* ✅ Strong cross-selling and upselling opportunities
* ✅ Solid base for developing collections

**⚠️ Risks:**

* Overdependence on a few SKUs
* Stock-out vulnerability
* Risk if trends change (vintage/shabby chic style)

**✅ Recommendations:**

**Catalog Optimization:**

1. **Permanent stock Top 20:** Zero stock-outs (target: 99% availability)
2. Develop **2–3 variants** per best-seller
3. Create themed bundles: “Tea Time”, “Romantic”, “Vintage Chic”
4. Analyze margins and prioritize most profitable products
5. Remove 20% of lowest-performing products (long tail)

**Purchasing & Pricing Strategy:**

1. Renegotiate supplier prices for Top 20 (-10 to -15%)
2. Test +5–10% price increase on best-sellers
3. Automated low-stock alert system
4. Pre-orders 6 months in advance for Top 20

**Marketing & Merchandising:**

1. Premium positioning (homepage, showcases, featured sections)
2. Lifestyle content (videos, inspiration photos, decor ideas)
3. Customer reviews as social proof (rating 4.5+)
4. Targeted ad campaigns (high ROI on best-sellers)

**📊 KPIs to Track:**

* Top 20 stock-out rate (<5%)
* Top 10 share of total revenue
* Best-seller inventory turnover (<30 days)
* Top product page conversion rate (+20%)

**💰 Estimated Impact:** Stock-outs reduced (-90%), margins +3–5%, additional revenue £300–500K

### 🌍 3. International Expansion Opportunity (Opportunity: Very High)

**📊 Key Data:**

* **UK:** 82% of revenue (£8.0M)
* **France:** 5% (£485K) – underexploited
* **Germany:** 4% (£390K) – high potential
* **Netherlands:** 2% (£195K)
* **Rest of Europe:** 5%
* **Outside Europe:** 2%

**🔍 Market Analysis:**

**🇫🇷 France**

* Mature decoration/gift market
* Cultural affinity with English shabby chic style
* High purchasing power
* Strong competition
* Language barrier

**🇩🇪 Germany**

* Largest European market
* Very high purchasing power
* Quality and design sensitive
* Strong Christmas traditions
* High quality expectations

**🇳🇱 Netherlands + 🇧🇪 Belgium**

* Medium-size but receptive markets
* Good standard of living
* Easy logistics

**✅ Recommendations:**

**Phase 1: UK Optimization (3–6 months)**
Strengthen the core market and increase customer lifetime value.

**Phase 2: France/Germany Testing (6–12 months)**

* Professional site translation
* Local pricing strategy
* Local logistics partnerships
* Targeted social media campaigns
* Micro-influencers
* Localized content
* Marketplace testing (Amazon, etc.)

**Phase 3: Scale (12–24 months)**
If revenue >£500K/year per country:

* Continental EU warehouse
* Local customer support
* Local product adaptation
* Retail partnerships
* Pop-up stores

**💰 Estimated Impact:** +£200K–£500K additional revenue in Year 2

---

### 👥 4. Customer Retention Optimization (Impact: High)

**📊 Key Data:**

* Unique customers: 4,370
* Total transactions: 20K
* Average: 5.9 transactions/customer
* Estimated repurchase rate: ~40%
* 60% buy only once
* Average LTV: £2,220 revenue / £800 net
* Average basket: £376

**🔍 Estimated Segmentation:**

* One-shot: 60%
* Occasional: 30%
* Loyal: 8%
* VIP/B2B: 2%

**✅ Recommendations:**

**1. Loyalty Program**
3 tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold) + points system

**2. Email Marketing & CRM**
RFM segmentation + automated email journeys (post-purchase, birthday, abandoned cart)

**3. Customer Experience Improvements**

* Simplified checkout
* Guest checkout
* Multiple delivery options
* Live chat, phone, WhatsApp support
* Premium packaging & unboxing

**4. Targeted B2B Strategy**

* Dedicated pricing
* 30-day payment terms
* Professional catalog
* Dedicated account manager

**📊 KPIs & 12-Month Targets:**

* Repurchase rate: 55–60%
* Purchase frequency: 7–8
* Churn rate: 45%
* Customer LTV: £1,200+

**💰 Estimated Impact:**

* Additional revenue: +£500K to £1M
* Loyalty program cost: £40–60K/year
* ROI: 10:1 to 15:1
* Breakeven: 3–4 months
